My Journey

How I learned to read data like a language

// 01

Numbers Were Boring — Until They Weren't

My first encounter with data analysis was a CSV file with thousands of rows. Opening it in Excel felt overwhelming. Then I loaded it with Pandas, ran `.describe()`, and suddenly patterns jumped out. Mean, median, outliers — the data had a story, and I just needed to know how to read it.

// 02

Visualization Changes Everything

A table of numbers tells you facts. A well-designed chart tells you what those facts mean. I learned that the hardest part of data analysis isn't the math — it's choosing the right visual to make the insight obvious at a glance. A good chart should make people say "oh, I see it now."

// 03

Data-Driven Products

Today I combine data analysis with my AI and Python skills to build products that get smarter over time. Dashboards that track KPIs, pipelines that surface anomalies automatically, reports that write themselves. Data isn't just analysis — it's the foundation of intelligent systems.
